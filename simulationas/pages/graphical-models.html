Graphical and Sequential Models

Reason about structure, not just isolated predictions.

Probabilistic graphs let you factor a complex distribution into local pieces. These labs focus on conditional independence, message passing, Markov dynamics, hidden-state decoding, and parameter learning.

Bayesian networks Markov random fields Belief propagation HMM decoding + learning
Lab 1

Conditional independence explorer

Choose a small graph pattern, mark observed variables, and inspect whether the path between the end nodes is active. This is the core logic behind d-separation in Bayesian networks.

    Lab 2

    Belief propagation on a simple factor graph

    Pass messages along a short chain and see how local evidence changes the marginal beliefs. On trees this procedure is exact; on loopy graphs it becomes an approximation.

    This lab uses a binary Markov random field. The compatibility number controls how strongly neighboring nodes prefer the same value.
      Lab 3

      Markov chains and hidden Markov models

      Edit transition and emission probabilities, then compute filtering probabilities, decode the most likely hidden sequence with Viterbi, and run one parameter-learning update from soft counts.
